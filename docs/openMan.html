The Manual Reference for OpenBSD in a Proper Sequence

OpenBSD don't give any reading sequence, assuming that the user is new to the system and therefor the sequence of reading the manuals are Free of sequence. But is really useful to know how to start, using their man pages that anyone can access them, offline or Online

The first man pages after booting the first time and reading the emails are this ones: man afterboot, man syspatch, man security, man intro, man hier, man passwd, man chpass and man kbd. For Date & Time, we have the man date and man ntpd. After that you may wish to read about the main Networking pieces to configure our system, that are man myname, man mygate, man hostname, man netstart, man ifconfig, man resolv.conf. To understand how to system boots and what service can run or not by defult, we read man init, man rc, man rc.conf & man rc.conf.local, man rcctl, man rc.d, man rc.subr and finally man reboot & man halt to now how to reboot or shutdown properly.
